Research

Research work focused on usable privacy, security awareness, Arabic-speaking users, and technology behavior in the Saudi context.

Users’ Awareness and Perceptions of Smartphone Privacy and Security Settings

Replication Study in Saudi Arabia

This research replicates and extends a prior smartphone privacy and security settings study in the Saudi context. It examines how Saudi smartphone users understand, configure, and evaluate privacy and security settings, with attention to awareness, default beliefs, past configuration, future intentions, perceived difficulty, effectiveness, usability impact, and privacy/security concerns.

The study surveyed 119 Saudi smartphone users and analyzed perceptions across 18 smartphone privacy and security settings. Findings highlight an awareness–behavior gap and suggest that privacy and security perceptions may be influenced by cultural expectations, institutional trust, and Arabic privacy terminology.
